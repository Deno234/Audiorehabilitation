# Evaluation of Language Generative Models for Auditory Rehabilitation Needs

Final report generated: 2026-06-04

## Abstract

This reproducible research project evaluated whether language generative models can produce Croatian words and short Croatian sentences suitable for auditory rehabilitation material under strict phoneme-class saturation constraints. Candidate text was generated by manual ChatGPT Plus prompting and local Ollama generation, but all acceptance decisions were made deterministically in Python using a Croatian phonemizer, target-class saturation metrics, duplicate and sentence-structure checks, Hunspell lexical screening, and manual word-level HJP review. Validated text was then synthesized with three local/free Croatian TTS systems and evaluated with WAV technical checks, local faster-whisper ASR WER/CER, and balanced human listening review. The main finding is that ChatGPT Plus substantially outperformed local Ollama for text generation, while eSpeak NG had the highest human intelligibility in listening review and SpeechT5 had the highest human naturalness.

## Research Question

Can LLM-based candidate generation produce Croatian words and 3-5 word Croatian sentences that satisfy deterministic phoneme-class saturation criteria for auditory rehabilitation exercises, and can validated candidates be converted into technically usable and intelligible audio?

## Reference-Paper Relation

The project reproduces the core idea from the reference paper: Croatian speech material generation for auditory training using five Croatian phoneme classes and saturation levels. The important methodological change is that the LLM is never trusted to count phonemes or validate its own output. The LLM only proposes candidate text; Python deterministically phonemizes, counts phonemes, computes saturation, detects failures, and writes reproducible CSV outputs.

## Croatian Phoneme Classes

- N / Niski: m, n, nj, b, p, u
- SN / Srednjeniski: v, g, o, h, l, lj
- S / Srednji: a, k, r, d, dž, f, ž
- SV / Srednjevisoki: č, e, š, t, đ, j
- V / Visoki: ć, i, c, z, s

Saturation formula: `target_class_phoneme_count / total_phoneme_count * 100`. Multi-letter Croatian phonemes `dž`, `lj`, and `nj` were parsed longest-match-first.

## Experimental Workflow

1. Generate candidate Croatian text with ChatGPT Plus manual prompts and local Ollama.
2. Validate text deterministically in Python.
3. Apply Hunspell screening as automatic lexical screening, not HJP proof.
4. Apply manual word-level HJP review and propagate word decisions to full candidates.
5. Select a balanced TTS subset from valid/HJP-reviewed candidates.
6. Synthesize the same text candidates with eSpeak NG, Coqui VITS HR, and SpeechT5 HR.
7. Validate WAV format and evaluate audio with local faster-whisper ASR WER/CER.
8. Add balanced human listening review for intelligibility and naturalness.

## Main Findings

1. Deterministic Python validation is essential. Both ChatGPT Plus and Ollama produced candidates that looked plausible but failed saturation, duplicate, lexical, or structural checks.
2. ChatGPT Plus was the best text-generation source in this experiment. Ollama `llama3.1:8b` was not reliable for strict Croatian phoneme saturation under the tested prompts.
3. Higher saturation levels generally increased difficulty, especially for sentences and difficult classes.
4. Duplicate generation was the main ChatGPT Plus weakness, while saturation failure was the main Ollama weakness.
5. HJP/manual word-level review reduced risk before audio synthesis and created a defensible TTS candidate pool.
6. All three TTS systems were technically usable once configured locally.
7. Human listening and ASR did not rank systems identically. ASR slightly favored SpeechT5, while human intelligibility strongly favored eSpeak NG. This supports the caveat that ASR WER/CER is useful but not sufficient.

## Limitations

- ChatGPT Plus generation was manual through the app, not API-automated.
- HJP validity was manually reviewed at word level; it was not scraped or automatically proven.
- Hunspell is lexical screening only and can be wrong for inflected or context-sensitive Croatian forms.
- Human listening review was performed on a balanced sample, not all audio files.
- ASR WER/CER is a proxy, not clinical proof of pronunciation quality.
- TTS output is not clinically approved; clinical suitability requires expert review and, ideally, listener studies.
- SpeechT5 used an available speaker embedding and should be interpreted as an experimental local setup.

## Future Work

- Add larger human listening review with multiple raters.
- Add inter-rater reliability if more reviewers score audio.
- Test stronger local LLMs or repair loops for Ollama-style generation.
- Expand Croatian lexical validation with a curated local word list or formal manual HJP audit.
- Optimize SpeechT5 loading so the model is not reloaded for every candidate.
- Create final clinician-facing candidate lists after semantic and clinical suitability review.
